The economy added 160,000 jobs in April, the Labor Department reported Friday, down from 208,000 jobs in March.
Unemployment was 5 percent, unchanged from March.
Analysts had predicted job growth of about 200,000 jobs, an unemployment rate of 4.9 percent and an increase in hourly earnings of 0.3 percent, according to a survey of Bloomberg economists.

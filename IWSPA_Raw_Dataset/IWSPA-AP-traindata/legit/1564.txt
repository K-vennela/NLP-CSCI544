Box Office: 'Transformers: Age of Extinction' takes Fourth of July
Los Angeles Times | July 6, 2014 | 11:56 AM
"Transformers: Age of Extinction" is the first summer flick to hold the No. 1 spot for two consecutive weeks, generating $53,792,000 for the five-day Fourth of July period and nearly $175 million after just 10 days of release. New Line Cinema's Melissa McCarthy-starrer "Tammy" landed in second after pulling in $32.9 million in its first five days.

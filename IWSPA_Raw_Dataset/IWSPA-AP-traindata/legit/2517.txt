Box Office: 'Ride Along' cruising to victory over 'Lone Survivor,' 'Jack Ryan'
Los Angeles Times | January 19, 2014 | 10:13 AM
It looks like Kevin Hart's "Ride Along" will easily lock up a first-place finish at the box office during the busy Martin Luther King Jr. Day weekend with an estimated $41.2 million and $47.8 million through Monday. Last week's No. 1 film, "Lone Survivor," will land in second place with an estimated gross of $23.2 million through Sunday, followed by animated 3-D critter caper "The Nut Job" with $20.5 million and Paramount Pictures' $60-million "Jack Ryan: Shadow Recruit" with $17.2 millio.

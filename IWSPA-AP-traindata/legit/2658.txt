Bernie Sanders has won the Indiana Democratic primary, according to three television networks.
The come-from-behind victory offers a much-needed boost for the Vermont senator, but Hillary Clinton still holds a commanding lead in the delegate race and has begun shifting her focus to a general election battle against Donald Trump.
Indiana awards its 83 pledged Democratic delegates on a proportional basis, with 56 allocated from district-level winners, 18 at-large delegates and nine pledged party officials and leaders - meaning that Sanders is unlikely to net many delegates.

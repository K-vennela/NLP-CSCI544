President Barack Obama will visit Hiroshima later this month, the White House announced Tuesday.
Hiroshima and Nagasaki were devastated by atomic bombs the U.S. dropped on the Japanese cities in 1945 during World War II.
Obama is scheduled to travel to Vietnam and Japan from May 21 through May 28 on his 10th trip to Asia. The White House said Obama will participate in his final G-7 Summit when he visits Japan.
"The President and Prime Minister Abe will meet bilaterally to further advance the U.S.-Japan alliance, including our cooperation on economic and security issues as well as a host of global challenges," the White House said. "Finally, the President will make an historic visit to Hiroshima with Prime Minister Abe to highlight his continued commitment to pursuing the peace and security of a world without nuclear weapons.
